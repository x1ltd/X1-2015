Describing a target is one of the most critical steps in a marketing program.

Description and target finding

Most marketers define their target by looking at the individuals that already buy from them.

In actuality, they are learning what is working for them and therefore trying to find individuals with similar characteristics to target. Proper description of a target takes a bit more thought, and you fail here you will be wasting precious marketing money on the wrong people.

You also need to look at the individuals that didn’t buy. The people that didn’t buy because you failed to communicate successfully with them.

The market is constantly changing – competitor values, the economy, even your own company’s unique selling points. These changes will alter the target description. If you only listen to data analytics to describe a target, you run the risk of describing a target of the past rather than the target of the present.
